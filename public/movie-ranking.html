The Best Movies According to Idara

My top 3 movies of all-time.

The Godfather (1972)

Directed by Francis Ford Coppola, this epic crime film stars Marlon Brando and Al Pacino as the leaders of a powerful New York crime family. It's praised for its screenplay, dramatic depth, and character development, and remains a powerful portrayal of the rise and fall of mob families in America.

Citizen Kane (1941)

Often topping lists of the greatest films of all time, Orson Wellesâ€™ masterpiece tells the life story of Charles Foster Kane (played by Welles himself), a wealthy newspaper magnate drawn from the life of William Randolph Hearst. The film is renowned for its innovative narrative structure, deep focus cinematography, and groundbreaking visual effects.

Schindler's List (1993)

Directed by Steven Spielberg, this historical drama recounts the true story of Oskar Schindler, a German businessman who saved the lives of more than a thousand mostly Polish-Jewish refugees during the Holocaust by employing them in his factories. It's noted for its emotional depth, historical accuracy, and stunning black-and-white cinematography.
